Why it works

The problem was never love. It was the calendar.

Life fills with the urgent, and the things you meant to do together — the trip, the class, the standing Sunday adventure — quietly slide to “later.” Later has a way of not arriving.

Researchers who study lasting couples keep landing on the same finding: the pairs who stay close are the ones who keep sharing new experiences — and who look forward to them, out loud, together. Anticipation is its own quiet happiness. Korby gives it somewhere to live.

  • Shared novelty
    Doing new things together reliably deepens connection — more than repeating the familiar.
  • Anticipation
    Looking forward to something with someone is a happiness of its own, before it even happens.
  • Savoring
    Returning to a good memory together makes it count twice.
  • Intention beats willpower
    A small shared system turns “we should” into a date on the calendar.
